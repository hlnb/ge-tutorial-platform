Where to Stay on Rottnest Island

Whether you're looking for luxury accommodation, a family-friendly cottage, or a camping adventure, Rottnest Island offers various options to suit all budgets and preferences.

Important: All accommodation must be booked through the official Rottnest Island Authority website. Book well in advance, especially for peak season (December-February) and school holidays.

üè® Hotel Rottnest

The island's premium accommodation option, Hotel Rottnest offers modern luxury in the heart of Thomson Bay Settlement. This boutique hotel features contemporary rooms with stunning ocean views.

Features

  • 80 modern hotel rooms
  • Ocean or garden views
  • On-site restaurant and bar
  • Swimming pool
  • Air conditioning
  • WiFi included
  • Daily housekeeping
  • Prime location near ferry

Room Types

  • Deluxe Room - Queen or twin beds, garden view
  • Premier Room - King bed, ocean view
  • Family Suite - Separate bedroom, sleeps 4
  • Luxury Suite - Spacious with balcony

Pricing (Indicative 2024)

Prices vary by season

  • Deluxe Room: From $350 per night
  • Premier Room: From $450 per night
  • Family Suite: From $550 per night
  • Luxury Suite: From $750 per night

Best for: Couples, special occasions, those seeking comfort and convenience

üè† Cottages & Villas

Self-contained accommodation options scattered around Thomson Bay Settlement and other locations across the island. Perfect for families and groups who want more space and flexibility.

Types of Cottages

Premium Villas
  • Modern, fully renovated properties
  • 2-4 bedrooms
  • Full kitchen and laundry
  • Air conditioning
  • Private outdoor areas
  • From $500 per night
Standard Cottages
  • Historic buildings with modern amenities
  • 1-3 bedrooms
  • Kitchen facilities
  • Fans (no air conditioning)
  • Charming, authentic experience
  • From $200 per night
Budget Cottages
  • Basic but comfortable
  • 1-2 bedrooms
  • Basic kitchen
  • Shared bathroom facilities in some
  • Great value for budget travelers

What to Bring for Cottages

  • All bed linen and towels (not provided)
  • Toiletries
  • Food and cooking supplies
  • Beach towels
  • Fans (summer months)

Best for: Families, groups, longer stays, self-catering

‚õ∫ Camping

For the adventurous, camping on Rottnest is an unforgettable experience. The designated camping area offers a unique way to connect with nature.

Camping Facilities

  • Designated camping ground near Thomson Bay
  • Tent sites and camper van sites
  • Shared bathroom and shower facilities
  • BBQ areas
  • No powered sites
  • Camp kitchen available

What You Need to Know

  • Bring your own tent and camping equipment
  • No campfires allowed (use designated BBQs only)
  • Food storage must be secure (quokkas!)
  • Book in advance - limited sites
  • Minimum 2-night stay during peak periods

Pricing

  • Tent site: $15 per person per night
  • Minimum: $30 per site per night

Best for: Budget travelers, nature lovers, adventurous families

üìÖ Booking Information

How to Book

  1. Visit the official Rottnest Island website
  2. Select your dates and accommodation type
  3. Check availability (book months ahead for peak times)
  4. Complete booking and payment online
  5. Receive confirmation via email

Booking Tips

  • Book Early: 6-12 months ahead for summer holidays
  • Shoulder Season: March-May and September-November for better availability
  • Weekdays: Easier to book than weekends
  • Cancellation Policy: Check terms before booking
  • Package Deals: Look for ferry + accommodation packages

Minimum Stays

  • Peak season (Dec-Feb): Usually 2-3 night minimum
  • School holidays: 2 night minimum
  • Off-peak: 1 night stays available
  • Some cottages: Weekly bookings only in summer

üí° Important Things to Know

Island Rules

  • No pets allowed (except registered assistance animals)
  • Smoking only in designated areas
  • No drones without permit
  • Quiet hours: 10pm - 7am
  • Take all rubbish with you

Limited Services

  • One general store (expensive - bring supplies from mainland)
  • Limited mobile phone coverage
  • No banking facilities (ATM available)
  • Limited medical facilities (nursing post only)
  • Few dining options (book restaurants ahead)

What to Pack

  • All food and drinks (for self-catering)
  • Sunscreen and hats
  • First aid supplies
  • Torch/flashlight
  • Insect repellent
  • Reusable water bottles
  • Entertainment (books, games)
  • Camera and chargers
